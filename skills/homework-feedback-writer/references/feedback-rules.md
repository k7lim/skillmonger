# Feedback Writing Rules

## The Five Principles

### 1. Specificity

Point to exact locations in the student's work. Name the problem precisely.

| Vague | Specific |
|-------|----------|
| "Grammar issues" | "Run-on sentence in paragraph 3, line 2" |
| "Needs more evidence" | "Your claim about climate change (p.2) has no citation" |
| "Unclear thesis" | "Your thesis has three ideas. Pick one." |

Test: If your comment could apply to any paper, rewrite it.

### 2. Assertion Over Hedging

Students need clarity. Hedging wastes their time and yours.

| Hedged | Direct |
|--------|--------|
| "You might want to consider..." | "Do this:" |
| "Perhaps this could be..." | "This is wrong because..." |
| "It seems like maybe..." | "You misunderstand X." |

Words to delete: somewhat, arguably, perhaps, a bit, tends to, might, could potentially, may potentially

### 3. Slop-Free Writing

These words signal AI-generated or corporate-speak text. Students won't trust feedback that sounds machine-written.

**Delete on sight:**
- delve, crucial, pivotal
- showcase, foster, landscape
- tapestry, groundbreaking
- utilize (use "use"), facilitate, leverage
- underscore (use "emphasize" or just make the point)

### 4. Brevity

Long feedback gets skimmed. Short feedback gets read.

**Target:** Under 25 words per feedback point.

| Verbose | Brief |
|---------|-------|
| "The thesis statement, while present, could benefit from additional specificity and clarity regarding the central argument you're attempting to make in this essay." | "Weak thesis. What's your actual claim?" |

### 5. Active Voice

Name who should do what. Passive voice hides responsibility.

| Passive | Active |
|---------|--------|
| "The thesis could be strengthened" | "Strengthen your thesis" |
| "Evidence should be added" | "Add evidence for your claim in paragraph 2" |
| "This section could be reorganized" | "Move paragraph 4 before paragraph 2" |

## Common Replacements

| Replace | With |
|---------|------|
| serves as | is |
| in order to | to |
| a wide variety of | many |
| due to the fact that | because |
| at this point in time | now |
| for the purpose of | to |
| in the event that | if |
| has the ability to | can |

## Feedback Structure

Good feedback typically follows this pattern:

1. **Location**: Where in the work
2. **Problem**: What's wrong (one sentence)
3. **Fix**: What to do about it (imperative)

Example: "Paragraph 2: No topic sentence. Add one that previews your three sub-points."
