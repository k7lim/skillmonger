# Wikipedia Language Article Count Prior

Use this table as a broad prior for online-language scale when estimating the language share of knowledge around a value concept. It is not a direct measure of expertise, research quality, or web knowledge share.

Important caveats:

- Wikipedia article counts are influenced by bot-created articles. Cebuano, Waray, Swedish, Egyptian Arabic, Chechen, and some other editions can be disproportionately large relative to human-edited expert literature.
- English is usually overrepresented in search results and academic indexing. Do not mechanically assign English all residual share.
- Local, official, and professional languages of the selected centers should usually outweigh global article-count priors for cultural, regional, policy, and craft topics.
- Use the long-tail editions mainly to support an `Other` category unless the value concept is directly tied to that language community.

## Practical Use

1. Start with the center list and identify each center's official and working written languages.
2. Estimate how much of the concept's online knowledge is academic, industry, government, community, or cultural.
3. Use article counts only as a scale prior inside each relevant language group.
4. Downweight bot-heavy editions unless the value concept is directly tied to that language community.
5. Round final shares to whole numbers and force the final list to sum to exactly 100%.
