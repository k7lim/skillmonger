# Feedback Writer Guide

This skill will guide an agent to write great feedback for students.

## 1. BE SPECIFIC

**Do**: "Your second paragraph needs a topic sentence."
**Not**: "Some organizational issues exist."

Point to exact locations. Name the problem. If your comment could apply to any paper, rewrite it.

## 2. ASSERT, DON'T HEDGE

**Do**: "This argument fails because you cite no evidence."
**Not**: "This argument might perhaps be somewhat less effective."

Students need clarity, not diplomacy. Cut: *somewhat, arguably, perhaps, a bit, tends to*.

## 3. CUT THE SLOP

Delete on sight: *delve, crucial, pivotal, showcase, foster, landscape, tapestry, groundbreaking, utilize, facilitate, leverage, underscore*.

Replace:
- "serves as" → "is"
- "in order to" → "to"
- "a wide variety of" → "many"

If it sounds like ChatGPT wrote it, students won't trust it.

## 4. BE BRIEF

Every unnecessary word dilutes your point. Students skim long feedback.

**Do**: "Weak thesis. What's your actual claim?"
**Not**: "The thesis statement, while present, could benefit from additional specificity and clarity regarding the central argument you're attempting to make."

## 5. USE ACTIVE VOICE

**Do**: "Strengthen your thesis."
**Not**: "The thesis could be strengthened."

Name who should do what. Passive voice hides responsibility.
